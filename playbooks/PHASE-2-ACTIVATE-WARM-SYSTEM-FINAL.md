## Component 4: MOMENTUM (Urgency Mechanisms That Accelerate Decisions)

### Goal
Stop waiting for leads to "get back to you." Engineer urgency into the activation process.

### The Psychology of Momentum

**Why Leads Stall:**

1. **Decision fatigue** - Too many options, analysis paralysis
2. **Future discounting** - "I'll deal with this later" (never happens)
3. **Status quo bias** - Doing nothing feels safer than acting
4. **Lack of consequences** - No cost to waiting

**The Momentum Solution:**

Create forcing functions that make action easier than inaction.

### Momentum Tactic #1: Time-Limited Access

**The Pattern:**

Offer something valuable with a deadline.

**Examples:**

**A. Bonus Expiration:**

"Book a demo this week â†’ Get [BONUS RESOURCE] (worth Â£XXX) free."

After this week, you can still book a demo, but the bonus goes away.

**B. Calendar Scarcity:**

"I've got 3 demo slots left this month. After that, we're booked until [NEXT MONTH]."

(Must be TRUE. Never fake scarcity.)

**C. Cohort Enrollment:**

"We're accepting 10 clients into our [PROGRAM NAME] cohort starting [DATE]. Applications close [DATE - 1 WEEK]."

**Why it works:**
- Fear of missing out (FOMO) > fear of making wrong choice
- Deadline = external forcing function (removes "I'll do it later" option)
- Scarcity increases perceived value

**Implementation:**

*Email Example:*
```
Subject: 3 demo slots left (this week)

[Name],

Quick heads-up:

I've only got 3 demo slots left this week.

After Friday, my calendar's blocked until [DATE].

If you've been thinking about [OUTCOME], now's the time.

[BOOK DEMO - LIMITED SLOTS]

If you miss the window, no worriesâ€”you can book for [NEXT MONTH], but I know how these things go... "later" becomes "never."

- [Your Name]
```

### Momentum Tactic #2: Engagement Ladders

**The Pattern:**

Stack micro-commitments that build to macro-commitment (sale).

**The Ladder:**

1. **Rung 1:** Download lead magnet (tiny commitment)
2. **Rung 2:** Reply to email (slightly bigger)
3. **Rung 3:** Complete assessment/quiz (medium commitment)
4. **Rung 4:** Watch webinar (bigger time investment)
5. **Rung 5:** Book demo call (near-buying commitment)
6. **Rung 6:** Purchase (biggest commitment)

**Why it works:**
- Commitment consistency principle (each yes makes next yes easier)
- Gradual investment (sunk cost fallacy kicks in)
- Reduces friction (big ask feels smaller after small yeses)

**Implementation:**

*Nurture Sequence with Escalating Asks:*

- Email 1: "Read this 2-minute tip" (passive consumption)
- Email 2: "Which one applies to you?" (reply ask)
- Email 3: "Take this 5-minute assessment" (data give)
- Email 4: "Watch this 15-minute training" (time give)
- Email 5: "Book a 30-minute strategy call" (calendar give)

Each step primes the next.

### Momentum Tactic #3: Live Events (Webinars/Workshops)

**Why Live Events Accelerate:**

- **Social proof** (others are here, must be valuable)
- **Commitment** (blocked calendar = invested attention)
- **Interactivity** (Q&A creates dialogue, not monologue)
- **Limited-time offer** (end of webinar = deadline)

## Troubleshooting

### Problem 1: Low Welcome Sequence Engagement

**Symptoms:**
- Email 1 open rate < 40%
- Email 2-5 open rates < 20%
- No replies

**Diagnosis:**
- Poor subject lines (too generic)
- Spam folder issues (deliverability)
- Wrong audience (lead source problem)

**Fix:**
- Test 5 subject line variants (curiosity vs. clarity)
- Check spam score (Mail-Tester.com)
- Audit lead source (are these real prospects or junk traffic?)
- Add reply ask in Email 1 (engagement signals help deliverability)

### Problem 2: Scoring Doesn't Predict Buying

**Symptoms:**
- Hot leads (51+ points) don't convert
- Cold leads (< 20 points) are buying
- Sales says "These aren't qualified"

**Diagnosis:**
- Scoring weights wrong (measuring activity, not intent)
- Fit criteria missing (behavioral score without ICP overlay)
- Time decay not implemented (old activity inflating scores)

**Fix:**
- Review closed deals: What behaviors did buyers exhibit?
- Adjust weights (e.g., pricing page visit = 20 points, not 5)
- Add fit multipliers (ICP match = 1.2x score)
- Implement time decay (no activity in 30 days = -50% score)
- Recalibrate quarterly (buying patterns change)

### Problem 3: Trigger Campaigns Get Low Response

**Symptoms:**
- Trigger emails have < 10% reply rate
- Leads click but don't respond
- Feels automated/generic

**Diagnosis:**
- Messaging is robotic (obvious template)
- Timing is off (too slow or too fast)
- CTA is unclear (what do they do next?)

**Fix:**
- Add personalization beyond name (reference specific behavior)
- Test send delays (immediate vs. 15 min vs. 1 hour)
- Use conversational tone ("Hey [Name], saw you..." not "Dear Valued Lead")
- Clear binary CTAs ("Reply YES or NO" vs. "Let us know your thoughts")

### Problem 4: Leads Stall at Warm Stage

**Symptoms:**
- 30-50% of leads get stuck at 30-50 points
- They engage but never reach hot status
- Long nurture cycles (> 60 days)

**Diagnosis:**
- No momentum mechanisms (passive nurture only)
- Missing micro-commitments (big jump from email click â†’ demo)
- Lack of urgency (no reason to act now)

**Fix:**
- Add engagement ladder (small yeses: reply â†’ quiz â†’ webinar â†’ demo)
- Introduce time-limited offers (scarcity)
- Run live events (webinars create deadline)
- Use multi-touch acceleration (increase frequency when they hit 40 points)

### Problem 5: High Unsubscribe Rate

**Symptoms:**
- > 1% unsubscribe rate per email
- Spam complaints
- List shrinking faster than it grows

**Diagnosis:**
- Frequency too high (daily emails = burnout)
- Relevance too low (generic blasts)
- Expectation mismatch (they expected X, got Y)

**Fix:**
- Segment by engagement level (send more to engaged, less to cold)
- Let leads choose frequency (weekly digest option)
- Deliver on promise (if lead magnet was about X, nurture about X)
- Use sunset policy (remove inactive leads after 90 days = cleaner list)
