## COMPONENT 1: CLARITY (Discovery Questions That Uncover True Pain)

### Goal
Transform "30-minute chat" into "deep diagnostic session" that reveals exactly why they're stuck—and makes them desperate for your solution.

### The Discovery Call Psychology

**The Problem with Generic Questions:**

Most salespeople ask surface-level questions:
- "What are your goals?" (Too vague, they don't know)
- "How much revenue do you want?" (Meaningless number)
- "What's your biggest challenge?" (They'll say "not enough leads" = obvious)

**Result:** No emotional engagement, no urgency, no close.

**The C.L.O.S.E Formula™ Approach:**

Ask **pain amplification questions** that:
1. **Quantify the problem** (how much is this costing you?)
2. **Expose consequences** (what happens if you don't fix this?)
3. **Build urgency** (why does this matter NOW?)
4. **Position you as the expert** (you've seen this before, you know how to fix it)

---

### The 7-Question Discovery Framework (UK Accountancy-Specific)

**Phase 1: Current State (Where Are They NOW?)**

**Q1: "Walk me through your current client acquisition process. How do new clients typically find you?"**

**What You're Listening For:**
- **Referral-dependent:** "95% referrals" = feast/famine cycle (vulnerable)
- **Failed experiments:** "Tried Facebook ads, wasted £3K" = skeptical (need proof)
- **No process:** "Word of mouth, I guess?" = flying blind (need structure)

**Follow-Up Questions (Drill Deeper):**
- "How many new clients do you get per month on average?"
- "How predictable is that? Can you forecast next month's new client volume?"
- "What happens during slow months? How do you handle the revenue gap?"

**What This Reveals:**
- Predictability (or lack thereof) = pain point #1
- Past failures = what NOT to do
- Current baseline = benchmark for results

---

**Q2: "You took The Catalyst Score™ and your bottleneck was [Phase]. Tell me—what's that actually costing you? Walk me through a month where you didn't hit your new client target."**

**What You're Listening For:**
- **Lost revenue:** "We need 5 new clients/month, only got 2 last month" = £30K+ lost opportunity
- **Stress/overwhelm:** "I panic when referrals dry up" = emotional pain (buying trigger)
- **Missed opportunities:** "Turned down work because too busy, then had a slow month" = poor pipeline management

**Follow-Up Questions (Amplify Pain):**
- "How many months per year does this happen?"
- "If you add up 12 months, what's the revenue gap between where you ARE and where you SHOULD BE?"
- "What would that extra revenue mean for you? (Hiring? Less stress? Exit planning?)"

**What This Reveals:**
- **Cost of inaction:** Quantify the problem (£50K-£200K lost annually)
- **Emotional drivers:** What they REALLY want (not just "more revenue")
- **Urgency:** Why they need to act now (not "someday")

**Example Exchange:**

**You:** "So you're saying you're at £600K revenue but should be at £1M based on your capacity?"

**Them:** "Yeah, we could easily handle 50% more clients with our current team."

**You:** "That's £400K sitting on the table. What would you do with an extra £400K?"

**Them:** "Hire 2 more staff, take some pressure off me, maybe actually take a holiday..."

**You:** "And how long have you been saying that?"

**Them:** "...3 years."

**You:** "So that's £1.2M you've left on the table while waiting for referrals to magically pick up. Does that sound about right?"

**Them:** (Realization moment) "...Yeah. Shit."

**→ This is where buying intent is created.**

---

**Phase 2: Desired Future (Where Do They WANT to Be?)**

**Q3: "Let's say it's 12 months from now. What does success look like? Paint me a picture of your practice if this problem was solved."**

**What You're Listening For:**
- **Revenue goal:** "We'd be at £1.2M" = target to reverse-engineer
- **Lifestyle goal:** "I'd work 4 days/week" = freedom motivation
- **Team goal:** "Hire 3 people, build a real business" = legacy motivation
- **Exit goal:** "Position for sale in 5 years" = financial motivation

**Follow-Up Questions:**
- "How many new clients per month would you need to hit that revenue?"
- "What would your week-to-week life look like? (Less stress? More time?)"
- "What would change for your team? Your family?"

**What This Reveals:**
- **Their real "why":** Money is means, not end (uncover true motivation)
- **Specific outcome:** You can reverse-engineer the path
- **Emotional buying trigger:** Logic sells, emotion buys

---

**Phase 3: The Gap (Why Haven't They Fixed It Yet?)**

**Q4: "If this has been a problem for [X months/years], what's stopped you from fixing it before now?"**

**What You're Listening For:**
- **"Too busy"** = Need turnkey solution (you do the work)
- **"Tried before, didn't work"** = Need proof it works (case studies, guarantees)
- **"Don't know where to start"** = Need roadmap (90-day plan)
- **"Too expensive"** = Need ROI framing (cost of inaction > cost of action)

**Follow-Up Questions:**
- "When you say 'too busy,' what would have to happen for this to become a priority?"
- "When you tried [Facebook ads/SEO/etc.], what went wrong?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and have this handled, what would that be worth to you?"

**What This Reveals:**
- **Objections:** Get them out NOW (before pitch) so you can address them
- **Budget:** "Worth £10K" = they can afford it
- **Decision criteria:** What do they value? (Speed? Proof? Support?)

---

**Phase 4: Consequences (What Happens If They Don't Act?)**

**Q5: "Let's play this out. What happens if we're having this same conversation 12 months from now and nothing's changed? You're still at £600K, still feast/famine, still stressed about pipeline. What does that cost you?"**

**What You're Listening For:**
- **Lost revenue:** "Another £400K gone" = financial pain
- **Burnout:** "I can't do this for another year" = emotional pain
- **Missed opportunities:** "Competitors will pull ahead" = fear of loss
- **Regret:** "I'll wish I'd acted sooner" = future regret (powerful motivator)

**Follow-Up Questions (Make It Real):**
- "How would that affect your family? Your health? Your team?"
- "What opportunities would you miss? (Hiring? Expanding? Selling?)"
- "At what point do you say 'enough is enough'?"

**What This Reveals:**
- **Cost of inaction:** Must be > cost of your solution (or no sale)
- **Urgency:** If status quo is unbearable, they'll act NOW
- **Motivation:** Fear of loss > desire for gain (use wisely)

**Example Exchange:**

**You:** "So if we're having this same conversation next year—still £600K, still no pipeline—what does that mean?"

**Them:** "Honestly? I might burn out. I'm already exhausted. Another year of this feast/famine cycle... I don't know if I can do it."

**You:** "And what happens to the practice if you burn out?"

**Them:** "...It falls apart. I'm the rainmaker. If I'm gone, it dies."

**You:** "So the real risk isn't 'will marketing work'—it's 'how long can I keep doing this before I break.'"

**Them:** "Yeah. Exactly."

**→ This is where urgency is created.**

---

**Phase 5: Solution Criteria (What Would the Perfect Solution Look Like?)**

**Q6: "If you could design the perfect solution—something that actually solves this without adding to your workload—what would it look like?"**

**What You're Listening For:**
- **"Done-for-you"** = They want to delegate (not DIY)
- **"Proven"** = Need case studies (risk-averse)
- **"Fast"** = Want results quickly (impatient)
- **"Not expensive"** = Budget constraint (address ROI)

**Follow-Up Questions:**
- "How much of your time could you realistically dedicate to this? (30 min/week? 2 hours?)"
- "What would 'success' look like in 90 days? (Lead volume? Revenue?)"
- "How do you make buying decisions for big investments? (Partner approval? Board?)"

**What This Reveals:**
- **Decision process:** Do they need partner buy-in? (If yes, get them on next call)
- **Timeline expectations:** "I need results in 30 days" = manage expectations
- **Budget:** "Not expensive" is relative—define what that means

---

**Phase 6: Authority & Timeline (Can They Buy? When?)**

**Q7: "Last question before I show you what we'd recommend: Are you the sole decision-maker, or is there someone else (partner, board) who'd need to sign off on this?"**

**What You're Listening For:**
- **"Just me"** = Can close on this call (decision-maker)
- **"Need partner approval"** = Get partner on next call (or call won't close)
- **"Need to think about it"** = Deflection (address real objection)

**Follow-Up Questions:**
- "If you love what I'm about to show you, is there any reason you couldn't move forward today?"
- "What's your timeline? Are you looking to start in the next 30 days, or is this a Q2/Q3 thing?"
- "What would need to be true for you to say 'yes, let's do this'?"

**What This Reveals:**
- **Close probability:** Can they buy today? (Or waste of time)
- **Real objections:** "Need to think" usually means price, skepticism, or not urgent
- **Timeline:** If "Q3," they're not ready (qualify out early)

### The Transition to Pitch

**After 7 Questions (30-40 Minutes), You Say:**

> "Okay, here's what I'm hearing:
>
> 1. **Current state:** You're at £600K but should be at £1M. The gap is costing you £400K/year. You've been stuck here for 3 years because referrals are unpredictable.
>
> 2. **Desired future:** You want £1.2M in 12 months, which means 10 new clients per month instead of 3-5. That would let you hire 2 staff and actually take a holiday for the first time in years.
>
> 3. **The gap:** You've tried Facebook ads (wasted £3K), you're too busy to figure out marketing, and you're skeptical because you've been burned before.
>

>
> 5. **Perfect solution:** Done-for-you, proven system that doesn't add to your workload. You've got 30 minutes/week max to dedicate to this. You need to see results in 90 days.
>
> **Does that sound accurate?"**

**Them:** "Yeah, that's spot on."

**You:** "Great. So here's what I'd recommend..."

**→ Now you pitch a solution tailored to THEIR EXACT SITUATION.**

**Why This Works:**
- **Mirror technique:** You repeated their words back = they feel heard
- **Summary = clarity:** They see their situation laid out clearly (often first time)
- **Permission to pitch:** "Does that sound accurate?" = they're bought into the problem (ready for solution)

## COMPONENT 3: OBJECTIONS (Pre-Handling Every Resistance Point)

### Goal
Address every objection BEFORE it becomes a reason to say no—and have scripted responses ready for the 20 most common UK accountancy objections.

### The Objection Psychology

**Why Objections Happen:**

1. **Fear of making wrong decision** (risk-averse accountants)
2. **Budget concerns** (marketing = expense mindset)
3. **Skepticism from past failures** (burned before)
4. **Decision fatigue** (too many options, easier to say no)
5. **Stall tactic** ("I need to think" = polite rejection)

**Traditional Approach: React to Objections**
- Wait for objection → try to overcome → feels combative → lose deal

**C.L.O.S.E Formula™ Approach: Pre-Handle Objections**
- Address objections BEFORE they arise → remove resistance → smooth close

---

## COMPONENT 4: SCARCITY (Creating Urgency Without Being Pushy)

### Goal
Give prospects a REASON to decide NOW (not "someday")—without feeling manipulative or salesy.

### The Scarcity Psychology (UK Accountancy Context)

**The Problem with "Take Your Time":**

Most salespeople say: "No pressure, take your time, let me know."

**Result:** 90% never respond (decision fatigue wins)

**The C.L.O.S.E Formula™ Approach:**

Create REAL scarcity (not fake) that makes delaying costly.

**3 Types of Scarcity That Work (Without Being Pushy):**

---

### Scarcity Type #1: Capacity Limits (Real)

**Framing:**

> "Quick heads-up: We cap new client onboarding at 3 practices per month.
>
> Why? Because Month 1 is setup-intensive (building the quiz, configuring Profit Voyager, training your team). If we take on more than 3, quality suffers.
>
> Right now, I've got 1 spot left in February and 2 spots in March.
>
> If you want to start in February (so you're seeing results by early April), we'd need to kick off this week. If March works, we've got a bit more time.
>
> **Which timeline makes more sense for you?"**

**Why It Works:**
- **Real constraint:** Not fake (you actually cap onboarding for quality)
- **Verifiable:** "Check my calendar" (they can see availability)
- **Consequence of delay:** Wait until March = results delayed by 4-6 weeks

---

### Scarcity Type #2: Time-Limited Bonus (Real Deadline)

**Framing:**

> "One more thing: If you sign up this week, I'll throw in the £1,500 setup fee for free.
>
> After Friday, it goes back to standard pricing (setup fee applies).
>
> **Why the deadline?** I'm launching a Q2 cohort on March 1st (5 practices starting together). If you're in that cohort, setup needs to happen in February.
>
> **So if you want the waived setup fee + Q2 cohort, we'd need to get contracts signed by Friday."**

**Why It Works:**
- **Real bonus:** £1,500 value (tangible)
- **Real deadline:** Q2 cohort starts March 1 (logical reason)
- **Soft pressure:** "If you want this" (not "you must decide now")

---

### Scarcity Type #3: Opportunity Cost (Lost Revenue)

**Framing:**

> "One last thing to consider: You said you're losing £400K/year by staying stuck at £600K revenue.
>
> **That's £33K/month.**
>
> If you start today, you'd see first results in Month 2-3 (late March / early April).
>
> If you wait 3 months to decide, you'd see results in Month 5-6 (June/July).
>
> **That's £100K in lost opportunity cost** (3 months × £33K).
>
> So the question isn't 'Is £12K expensive?'—it's 'Can I afford to lose another £100K while I think about it?'
>
> **Does that make sense?"**

**Why It Works:**
- **Quantifies delay:** £100K lost (makes procrastination painful)
- **Their own words:** You're using THEIR numbers (£400K/year loss)
- **Logical pressure:** Not "buy now because I say so" but "math says waiting is expensive"

---

### What NOT to Do (Fake Scarcity = Destroys Trust)

**❌ DON'T SAY:**
- "This price expires at midnight!" (unless it actually does)
- "I've only got 1 spot left!" (if you don't actually cap)
- "Someone else is about to take this slot!" (unless true)

**Why Fake Scarcity Backfires:**
- UK accountancy market is small (word spreads fast)
- Accountants are analytical (they'll fact-check you)
- Damages long-term reputation (not worth one sale)

**✅ DO SAY:**
- "I cap onboarding at 3/month for quality" (verifiable, logical)
- "Q2 cohort starts March 1" (real deadline, tied to operations)
- "You're losing £33K/month in opportunity cost" (their own math, not your manipulation)

## ADVANCED CLOSING TACTICS

### Tactic #1: The "Assume the Sale" Close

**When to Use:** Lead is engaged, asking detailed questions, no major objections

**How It Works:**
Don't ask "Do you want to move forward?"
Instead, ask "When do you want to start?"

**Example:**

**You:** "So that's the full system. Tier 2 makes sense for you—£12K, 6 months, full Momentum Loop™. When do you want to kick off—this week or next week?"

**Them:** "Uh, this week works."

**You:** "Perfect. I'll send the contract now. Give me your email again?"

**→ You closed without asking "yes or no"**

---

### Tactic #2: The "Takeaway" Close

**When to Use:** Lead is hesitant, not showing urgency

**How It Works:**
Create scarcity by pulling back the offer

**Example:**

**You:** "You know what, I'm actually not sure we're the right fit. You mentioned you're in busy season and don't have time to focus on this. Maybe we should revisit in Q3 when things slow down?"

**Them:** "No, wait—I DO want to do this. I just... I'm nervous about the time commitment."

**You:** "Okay, so it's not timing—it's the time commitment concern. Let's address that..."

**→ You uncovered the real objection by taking the sale away**

---

### Tactic #3: The "Referral" Close

**When to Use:** Lead is perfect fit but says no (budget, timing, etc.)

**How It Works:**
If they won't buy, ask for a referral

**Example:**

**You:** "No worries, I totally understand the budget's tight right now. Before we hang up, quick question: Do you know any other UK accountancy practice owners who ARE ready to invest in growing their practice? I'd love a warm intro if you know anyone."

**Them:** "Actually, yeah—my mate runs a practice in Leeds. He's been complaining about the same feast/famine problem."

**You:** "Perfect. Can you intro us via email? I'll send you a template you can forward."

**→ You didn't get the sale, but you got a warm lead**
